Slow the Curve: Stay Home

How Corona Spreads

The Coronavirus (also known as COVID-19) is a virus that is extremely contagious, and even though 80% of the cases are mild, 20% of the those infected have a serious case and might need to be hospitalized. Another reason that COVID-19 spreads so easily is because of how people can be carriers before they realize it. Those infected usually show symptoms around 5 days after they get infected, but they can infect others long before they show symptoms. The new people infected then don't realize they are infected, and proceed in their normal lives, leading to the infection of much more people since people don't realize it until they show symptoms.

How to fight boredom while staying home

One of the most important things to remember is to keep up a good daily routine. This way your body won't fall out of its regular sleep/wake schedule. It is also important to go outside and do some physical activity every day. Getting outside will make you feel better and improve your mental state while keeping you more fit.

Some possible activities that you could do outside while keeping a good social distance from others

  • Running/Walking
  • Biking

Other things you could do to spend the time while practicing social distancing include:

  • Board games with other members of your household
  • Online conferences with friends
  • Spend the extra time from not commuting to learn a new skill or complete a project that you have been procrastinating on for a while.

Social Distancing

The solution to this problem of people not realizing they are infected is something called social distancing. This works to slow the spread of the virus because all people stay away from each other, preventing the exponential spreading by limiting the interactions of everyone, preventing the exponential spreading.
